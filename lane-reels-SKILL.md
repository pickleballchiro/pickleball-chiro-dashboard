# Lane Reels Skill

## Purpose

Generate Instagram Reels for @dr.lane_o in one of two content modes:

**MODE 1 — ENGAGEMENT/CONVERSION (loop-opening):** Opens a curiosity loop, mirrors Dave's experience, creates desire without completing the explanation. Dave feels understood and curious. Used for: plateau content, identity hooks, belief violations, mechanism teases, conversion posts. CTA: "Comment READY and I'll reach out about a session."

**MODE 2 — TEACHING/VALUE (coaching demonstration):** Gives a genuine technique tip or coaching insight. Real, actionable instruction. Dave sees Lane coach well and trusts him enough to book. The gap is "knowing this vs. executing it under real match pressure" — that's what a session closes. CTA: "If you want to work on this specifically, comment READY."

All reels:
1. Route to **READY** as the primary ManyChat keyword (not yet live — write toward it).
2. Are built to beat **the 3-second hold rate** — the single most important algorithmic signal in 2026.
3. Follow the correct arc for the chosen mode (see below).
4. Use Lane's peer-to-peer brand voice — never clinical, never lecture-y.

## STEP 2: Choose the Reel Type and Sub-Format

### TYPE A — B-ROLL REELS

B-roll reels are court footage / gameplay clips with text overlays. No voiceover. The text IS the content. These are reach-maximizers — algorithm-first, designed to pull non-followers into the funnel.

**How it works:**
- Line 1 appears in the first 1–2 seconds. This is the hook. It must stop the scroll before audio registers.
- Line 2 appears 2–4 seconds later. This is the gap-widener or secondary hook. It deepens curiosity and directs to the caption.
- Caption does the heavy lifting — emotional mirror + gap statement + keyword CTA.

**B-Roll Sub-Formats:**

| Format | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Two-Line Hook** | Line 1: Hook (≤7 words). Line 2: Gap-widener or CTA (≤12 words). Clean, fast, high reach. | Default format — use for 80% of B-Roll |
| **Paragraph Overlay** | Single longer text block (3–6 sentences). Works when Dave will stop and actually read. Creates loop through watch time. | Use sparingly — when the insight is surprising enough that reading = watching. Clinic observations, counterintuitive truths. |

Read `references/broll-overlay-rules.md` for exact copy constraints per format.

---

### TYPE B — TALKING HEAD REELS

Lane speaks directly to camera OR overlays himself (background removed) onto B-roll footage. These build parasocial trust and credibility faster than any other format. The face builds the brand.

**How it works:**
- Spoken script is 30–45 seconds. Shorter is always better — aim for 30 sec first.
- Header text overlay: hook phrase (4–7 words, Oswald Bold) appears at top of frame.
- Bottom text: keyword CTA appears at the end or throughout bottom of frame.
- Caption is shorter than B-Roll caption — 5–7 lines, max 2 sentences per line.

**Talking Head Sub-Formats:**

| Format | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Straight to Camera** | Lane speaks directly. No movement, no B-roll behind. Raw, credible, peer-to-peer. | Opinion takes, belief violations, "I see this every week in the clinic" type content |
| **B-Roll Overlay** | Lane (background removed) overlays onto gameplay / court footage. More production but same script structure. | Performance/technique content where the visual context adds meaning |
| **Call-Out Reel** | Opens with "This is for every [specific Dave identifier]..." Hyper-niche, self-selecting, high watch time from target audience. | When targeting a very specific symptom, player type, or situation |

Read `references/talking-head-script-rules.md` for the script format and spoken word rules.

## STEP 3: Write the Content

### For B-Roll Reels

**Two-Line Hook format:**

```
LINE 1 (Hook): [4–7 words. Oswald Bold. Stops scroll before audio.]
LINE 2 (Gap): [≤12 words. Deepens curiosity OR names the caption as where the answer lives.]
```

**Paragraph Overlay format:**

```
[Opening sentence — hook. 8–12 words max.]
[Sentence 2 — validates. Names why Dave's attempts failed.]
[Sentence 3 — the gap. A specific answer exists. Never what it is.]
[Optional sentence 4 — points to caption or keyword.]
```

**Rules for Mode 1 B-Roll overlay copy:**
- Line 1 MUST pass the "Is this exactly me?" test for Dave. Generic = skip.
- Never complete the explanation. The curiosity loop must stay open.
- No sentence explains the mechanism. Name the category. Never the answer.
- If the overlay teaches Dave something he can act on without watching → rewrite it.

**Rules for Mode 2 B-Roll overlay copy:**
- Line 1 states the topic. Can hint at the tip. "Here's why your reset pops up."
- Line 2 can add specificity: "Most players get the paddle angle wrong — not the swing path."
- Still leave "executing this under real match pressure" as the gap that a session fills.
- Max 4 sentences total across all overlay text.
- No hyphens — commas and colons only.

### For Talking Head Reels

**Script structure — MODE 1 (Engagement/Conversion):**

```
[HOOK — 1 sentence. Stops the scroll. Call-out or belief violation.
"If your reset keeps popping up no matter how much you drill it — watch this."
"Your third shot isn't inconsistent because of your swing."]

[MIRROR — 1–2 sentences. Name Dave's experience precisely: the specific shot failure, the plateau pattern, what he keeps losing to. Not what's wrong technically — what he experiences and feels.]

[VALIDATION — 1–2 sentences. Name why his past attempts failed. No blame. Just truth.
"You've drilled it. You've watched the breakdown. Works in practice. Falls apart in a real point."]

[THE GAP — 1 sentence. A specific, fixable cause exists. NEVER name it.
"The reason it breaks down under pressure has nothing to do with your swing."]

[CTA — "Comment READY and let's work on this." Direct. No fluff.]
```

**Script structure — MODE 2 (Teaching/Value):**

```
[HOOK — 1 sentence. State the topic. Can be direct: "Here's why your reset keeps popping up — and the actual fix."]

[TEACH — 2–3 sentences. Give the real mechanic. Be specific. Be genuinely useful.
"Most players' resets pop because they're reaching for the ball instead of moving their feet to it. When you let the ball come to you and absorb with a slightly open face, the net clears every time."]

[THE REAL GAP — 1 sentence. Executing under match pressure is different from knowing.
"The drill is simple. Getting it to hold when you're under fire at the kitchen — that's what we work on in a session."]

[CTA — "If you want to work on this specifically, comment READY and I'll reach out." Organic. Not a pitch.]
```

**Script rules:**
- Total word count: 75–115 words (≈30–45 sec at natural speaking pace)
- Every sentence is one idea. No run-ons.
- Write for SPOKEN word — short punchy sentences, contractions, natural pauses.
- The hook is the first sentence, not a preamble. Start speaking into the camera with the hook.
- Never use clinical language in the script (no "medial compartment," "patellofemoral," etc.)
- Never use coaching jargon as the hook (no "kinetic chain," "proprioception," etc.) — Dave doesn't talk like that
- Lane's voice: direct, peer-to-peer, slightly intense. Not a lecture. Not a sales pitch. A conversation with a player who gets it. He plays at 5.0 and he's watched your specific game fall apart — he knows exactly what's happening.

## STEP 4: Write the Caption

### B-Roll Caption Structure:

```
[Line 1: Mirror the overlay hook — max 125 chars. Must work standalone before "More."]

[Narrator entry: 1–2 sentences. Lane's "I" — why he's paying attention to this. Real experience. Never preamble.]

[Emotional mirror: 2–4 short lines. Dave's specific experience named. No mechanism.]

[The Gap: 1 sentence. A specific answer exists. Never what it is.]

Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll [specific framing — access to clarity, not "buy my thing"]

[Engagement question — about Dave's experience, never his knowledge]
```

### Talking Head Caption Structure (shorter):

```
[Line 1: Mirror the hook from the script — max 125 chars]

[2–3 short lines: Emotional mirror. What Dave is experiencing. One line per beat.]

[The Gap: 1 sentence.]

Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll [specific framing]

[Single engagement question]
```

**Universal caption rules:**
- Mode 1: if the caption gives the mechanism → rewrite it. If Dave can solve from the caption alone → rewrite it. The job: make Dave feel seen and incomplete.
- Mode 2: real insight is allowed. Caption CAN include the tip. The job: make Dave feel seen and trust Lane's coaching. Close with the organic READY CTA.
- Both modes: end with a real engagement question Dave has an answer to.

## STEP 5: The Hormozi Invisible-Influence Layer

After writing the copy, run this mental check before presenting to Lane:

**The decision architecture test:**

Dave does not feel sold to when:
1. The hook mirrors his exact internal experience (he feels understood)
2. The content names what failed — not what to do (he feels validated, not lectured)
3. The gap is real and specific — he didn't know the gap existed (he feels curious)
4. The CTA feels like access to clarity, not a purchase (commenting feels like a smart player move, not a buyer move)

When all four are true, Dave DMs Lane because he decided to — not because he was pitched to. That's the principle. Every reel either achieves this or it doesn't. There's no middle.

**Ask before presenting:**
- Does this reel make Dave feel understood first?
- Does it sell the curiosity of the gap — not the product?
- Is the CTA framed as a player move ("players who are serious about fixing this") rather than a purchase prompt?

## Quick Reference: The Non-Negotiables

**Voice:** Direct, peer-to-peer, credible. Lane is the coach who plays at 5.0 AND watches the body underneath the shot. Not a lecturer. Not a clinic. Not a generic coach who learned pickleball at 50. The high-level competitor who can see exactly what's holding Dave's game back — and can also see what nobody else caught about what his body is doing.

**Content priority:** 70% skill/technique/coaching. 20% performance/movement. 10% max pain/longevity. Every reel defaults to coaching and technique unless specified otherwise.

**The selling philosophy:** Content sells decisions, not information. Dave doesn't have a knowledge deficit — he has a solution deficit. Every reel is a decision engine. Not a resource center. Not an education channel. For coaching content: Dave arrives at "DM me to work on this" as his own conclusion because the reel named exactly what he's struggling with and made it clear Lane can fix it.

**The Hormozi principle:** The best sale is the one the buyer initiates. Engineer the recognition, the validation, and the curiosity gap with enough precision that Dave arrives at "Comment LESSONS" or "DM me" as his own conclusion — not a response to a pitch.

**The one test:** After watching this reel and reading the caption, does Dave feel *understood and curious* — or *educated and satisfied*? Understood and curious books lessons. Educated and satisfied scrolls.

**The algorithm rule:** 3-second hold rate is everything. If the first line of overlay text or the first spoken sentence doesn't stop the scroll before audio registers, the reel will underperform regardless of what follows. Hook first. Always.
